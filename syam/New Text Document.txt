A blockchain is powered by five core components that work together to create a secure, decentralized, and immutable system.Distributed Ledger: A shared, immutable record of all transactions. It consists of sequential blocks—each storing transaction data, a timestamp, and a cryptographic hash of the previous block.Peer-to-Peer (P2P) Network: A decentralized network of computers (nodes) that maintain copies of the ledger. Nodes communicate directly to verify transactions without requiring a central server.Consensus Mechanism: Protocols (such as Proof of Work or Proof of Stake) that enable all nodes to agree on the validity of transactions. This ensures a single source of truth without central authority.Cryptography: Employs cryptographic hashes for tamper-proofing blocks and uses public/private key pairs for secure digital signatures, ensuring data integrity and user privacy.Smart Contracts: Self-executing code stored on the blockchain. They automatically trigger specific actions or transfers of value when pre-defined conditions are met.For a deeper dive into the exact mechanics of these elements, explore the comprehensive Introduction to Blockchain Technology guide on GeeksforGeeks.
